Causal Inferences and Uplift Models on Finance Data

Causal inference is a methodological approach aimed at discerning the cause-and-effect relationships between variables. It involves a meticulous analysis to predict the outcomes that may result from taking a specific action, shedding light on the potential consequences of implementing action A. Uplift modeling is a specialized application within this framework, concentrating on predicting the incremental impact of treatments for targeted interventions. My interest in this analysis sparked when contemplating the impact of applying a treatment A within the realm of loan lending business. I am eager to delve deeper into its capabilities and uncover its potential applications in financial data as I continue my work.

Derivative-Free Optimizations

My current research in ML is derivative-free optimizations method, this is also known as gradient-free optimization. Usually we will use it when gradients of objective functions are unavailable. I found this problem when I tried to use RL to train agents using simulated points, and realized that derivative-free optimizations could be an easier and simpler approach.
